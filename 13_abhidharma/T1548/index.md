<h1>The Higher Teaching of Śāriputra</h1>
<h4>(S. Śāriputra Abhidharma; C. <span class="ch">舍利弗阿毘曇論</span>)</h4>
<hr/>
<div class="intro"><p><cite>The Higher Teaching of Śāriputra</cite> was translated to Chinese by Dharmayaśas and Dharmagupta in 414 CE. It appears to have served as the main part or the entire Abhidharma Pitaka of the Dharmaguptaka school, given its structure and agreement with the Chinese <cite>Dīrgha Āgama</cite>.</p>

<p>The collection was divided into four divisions, which contain a total of 33 chapters. It spans 195 pages of the <em>Taisho</em> edition, which would yield approximately 234,000 words if fully translated to English.</p>
<p>The first two divisions are generally parallel to content found in the <cite>Vibhaṅga</cite> and <cite>Dharmaskandha</cite> texts from the Theravāda and Sarvāstivāda Abhidharma traditions. The third division contains material with a similar format as in the <cite>Dhātukatha</cite> and <cite>Dhātukāya</cite>. The last division further discusses a selection of topics like <em>saṃskāra</em>, name and form, contact, causation, and <em>samādhi</em>.</p>
