<h1><a href='../index.html'>The Related Discourses</a></h1>
<h2><a href='index.html'>5. Elements</a></h2>

<table class="trans">
  <th class='ch'>（四四八）有偈</th>
  <th class='en'>5 (448). With Verse</th>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t99.2.115b24'>如是我聞： 一時，佛住王舍城迦蘭陀竹園⋯如上廣說已⋯即說偈言：</td>
    <td id='p1'>1. Thus I have heard:<sup id="ref1"><a href="#n1">1</a></sup> One time, the Buddha was staying at the Squirrel Bamboo Park of Rājagṛha  … <em>recited in full as in SĀ 5.4 above</em> … He then spoke these verses:</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t99.2.115b26'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>「常會故常生；<br/>
    相離生則斷。<br/>
    如人執小木<br/>
    而入於巨海，<br/>
    人木則俱沒，<br/>
    懈怠俱亦然。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>“One is always born with those one always associates;<br/>
    Separated from each other, birth then ends.<br/>
    Like someone who clings to a small log<br/>
    And enters the vast ocean,<br/>
    When the person and log both sink,<br/>
    It’s the same when both are lazy.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t99.2.115b29'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>當離於懈怠，<br/>
    卑劣之精進。<br/>
    賢聖不懈怠；<br/>
    安住於遠離。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>One should part with someone lazy<br/>
    Whose effort is very weak.<br/>
    The noble person isn’t lazy;<br/>
    They stand securely far from it.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t99.2.115c2'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>慇懃精進禪，<br/>
    超度生死流。<br/>
    膠漆得其素，<br/>
    火得風熾然。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>They diligently make effort in meditation,<br/>
    And ford across the stream of birth and death.<br/>
    Sticking together, they obtain their brightness,<br/>
    As a fire burns hot in the wind.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t99.2.115c4'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>珂乳則同色，<br/>
    眾生與界俱。<br/>
    相似共和合，<br/>
    增長亦復然。」</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>Shells and milk have the same color,<br/>
    As beings go with their elements.<br/>
    Resembling each other, they unite<br/>
    And grow larger in the same way.”</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
</table>
